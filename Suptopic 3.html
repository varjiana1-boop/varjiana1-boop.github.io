URL ADR in Film: Everything You Need to Know About the Process
Site Author Alyssa Miller
Abstract This article discusses ADR, also known as automated dialogue replacement. This is a technique that is used in post production to change dialogue in the event of a change in story or issues when originally recording a piece of dialogue. Usually it happens when there is a noisy background of a recording and it cannot be used when editing. Therefore, in ADR, the dialogue will be re-recorded in a quieter environment. However, this article does not only talk about why ADR gets recorded, it goes into how it is actually done and tips on how to do it successfully. Some tips mentioned include knowing which scenes need ADR, preparing the space where it will be recorded, find the best microphone replacement, preparing the actor for this recording, and getting multiple takes just in case one does not go well.

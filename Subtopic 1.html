URL The Magic AI in Pre-Production: Making the Invisible Visible
Site Author Communication team of numalis.com
Abstract This article talks about the pre-production stage of the filmmaking process and how Artificial Intelligence fits into all of it. It goes over how AI can both positively and negatively impact the pre-production process. It discusses how AI can be used for script breakdowns, scriptwriting, budget estimations, and even to predict the success of your film. However, it also discusses how AI can limit creativity in terms of screenwriting as people have been relying on AI to do the work for them as of late, and it takes away the opportunity for people to use their minds and be creative. It also has ethical problems as it can take away jobs from people in the pre-production field. There also is the potential for transparency issues as producers use AI to make decisions, yet AI is not always 100% accurate.
URL "How Screenwriters Can Ethically Use AI" Insights from Screenwriter Andy Bobrow and WriterDuet Found Guy Goldstein
Site Author Brock Swinson
Abstract This article is an interview with Andy Bobrow, a tv writer and producer, and the founder of WriterDuet, Guy Goldstein. They talk about how AI has affected the screenwriting process specifically. They agree that AI is great for summarizing a script and making sure that the story is coming across the way that you want it to. With that being said, that’s all they think AI should be, the assistant, not the sole writer. They agree that whenever AI writes a script entirely, it lacks the human element needed to make the story good and worth reading. They also touch upon the issue of copyright and intellectual property and how that can become a problem with AI.
